‘Get a financial personal trainer’: the 10 new rules for money management

Today, money management goes beyond Charles Dickens’s much-quoted lesson, as set out in David Copperfield: “Annual income twenty pounds, annual expenditure nineteen and six, result happiness. Annual income twenty pounds, annual expenditure twenty pounds ought and six, result misery.”
So what simple rules can today’s would-be savers and investors follow to ensure they can enjoy the present while also futureproofing their finances?
The 50-30-20 rule: the basics of budgeting It may sound like another dietary fad, but 50-30-20 is about getting your finances, rather than your body, into shape. A useful rule of thumb, popularised by a range of online money guides, is the 50-30-20 rule. According to the rule: 50% of your income after tax should go to needs, such as your mortgage or rent, food, utilities, commuting costs, insurance, minimum monthly debt repayments and childcare; 30% to flexible expenses – your wants or “nice-to-haves” – which may include streaming subscriptions, flat whites and holidays and whatever else gives you pleasure; and 20% set aside for savings, investments and additional payments to reduce debt (on top of your minimum monthly debt repayments – which come out of needs).
